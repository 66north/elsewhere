The 4M40 diesel uses four glow plugs for cold starting — they pre-heat the combustion chambers so that compressed air is hot enough to ignite diesel fuel without a spark. A failed glow plug causes difficult cold starting, white smoke on cold start that takes longer than usual to clear, and rough running until the engine warms up. Glow plugs are consumable items and should be inspected or replaced at 80,000–100,000 km intervals or whenever cold starting becomes difficult.

## Symptoms

- Hard starting in cold weather (engine cranks but is reluctant to fire)
- White smoke on cold start that takes more than 2 minutes to clear
- Rough misfiring idle when engine is cold that smooths out when warm
- Glow plug warning light stays on longer than usual before cycling off
- One cylinder consistently misfires on cold start (single failed plug)

## Tools needed

- Glow plug socket (typically 10 mm hex)
- Extension bar and ratchet
- Torque wrench
- Penetrating oil (WD-40 or equivalent)
- Multimeter (for testing before removing)
- Anti-seize compound (for installation)

## Tips

- Use anti-seize on the new plug threads to ensure the next set can be removed without risk of breakage.
- Glow plug relay failure (not the plugs themselves) is also common and produces identical symptoms. Test the relay before replacing all plugs.
- On the 4M40, the glow plug relay is typically in the engine bay fuse/relay box. It can be tested by swapping with an identical relay.
- If the engine starts well in warm weather but is hard to start only in winter, the plugs are likely marginal — not completely failed. Replace before winter arrives.
