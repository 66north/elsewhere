The Langjökull highlands in early spring are unforgiving. Fresh snow on the plateau, roads barely passable, but the Pajero handled it with grace. We pushed north from Reykjavík toward the interior, following Route 1 and then veering onto the F-roads that cut through the heart of Iceland.

## Day 1: Route 1 to the Interior

The drive from Reykjavík took longer than expected—snow conditions deteriorated as we climbed elevation. The Pajero's all-terrain tires found purchase where lesser vehicles would struggle. We made camp near Deildartunguhver, where hot springs steam against the arctic backdrop.

## Day 2: Langjökull Plateau

F39 was challenging but rewarding. The plateau stretches endlessly, broken only by geothermal vents and the occasional fox. We navigated by landscape more than GPS. The weather cleared for a few hours—long enough to see the Langjökull glacier from a distance and understand the scale of what we were crossing.

## Day 3: Descent & Geysir

On the way back, we detoured to Haukadalur Valley. Geysir itself was quiet, but the geothermal field around it—Strokkur erupting every few minutes, the smell of sulfur, the sound of boiling water beneath the earth—reminded me why this country demands respect.

The Pajero's suspension handled every washboard section and river crossing. At 248,310 km on the odometer, it's still proving itself.

**Gear:** Frontiermen camp stove, MSR Dromedary bags for water, extra fuel (no stations on F39), sat communicator.

**Rig status:** All systems nominal. Added skid plate protection before the trip. No issues.
