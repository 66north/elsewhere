Door sag, wind noise, and poor sealing on the Pajero IV are usually caused by hinge wear or striker misalignment rather than structural damage. The factory manual documents adjustment procedures for door clearance, door height, hood (bonnet) height and striker engagement, and the back door (tailgate). All are adjustable within a range using slotted mounting points — no specialist tools required. Door handle play checks are also part of the Body section.

## Symptoms

- Door drops when opened and does not align with the body when closing
- Door requires lifting to close properly (sagging hinge)
- Wind noise when driving — door not seating fully in the seal
- Door striker clicking or chattering over bumps (striker engagement too loose)
- Tailgate (back door) difficult to close or has excessive play
- Hood (bonnet) sitting higher on one side or not latching flush
- Door handle has excessive free travel before triggering the latch

## Tools needed

- T40 Torx socket (hinge bolts on some models)
- 13mm socket (striker bolts)
- Marker pen (to mark striker contact point)
- Torque wrench

## Tips

- Door sag is caused by worn hinge bushings or pins. Before adjusting the striker, check the hinges: open the door to 45 degrees and lift the door edge — if it moves more than 3 mm vertically, the hinge pins or bushings are worn and should be replaced before adjusting the striker.
- Striker adjustment: the door striker is on a slotted plate and can be moved up/down and in/out. Loosen the striker bolts, close the door slowly and observe where the latch contacts the striker, then shift the striker to centre the contact. Use a marker pen on the striker to see the contact point.
- Hood adjustment: the hood hinge is bolted to the body with slotted holes — loosen both hinge bolts on the affected side and reposition. The hood striker (the pin the latch hooks over) is also adjustable for height — turn it in or out to achieve flush fitment.
- Back door fit: the tailgate hinges, striker, and check strap are all adjustable. If the back door droops when open, the check strap (the device that holds the door at full-open) may be stretched or the hinge bushings worn.
- Door seal compression: a correctly adjusted door compresses the seal evenly around the perimeter. If you can pull a piece of paper out from between the closed door and the seal without resistance, the door is not sealing at that point — adjust the striker inward.
- Door handle play: the factory specification for outside handle play is 0–2 mm of free movement before the latch begins to open. If handle play is greater than this, the handle-to-latch rod may be out of adjustment or the handle mechanism is worn.
- After any door adjustment, always test the door lock and unlock function before reassembling trim — the central locking actuator rod routing can be disturbed if the door card is removed.
