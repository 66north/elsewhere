Starting and charging faults are among the most common reasons a Pajero IV won't start or dies unexpectedly. The system has three key components: the battery (stores energy), the alternator (recharges while running), and the starter motor (cranks the engine). A fault in any one of them produces similar symptoms — this guide walks through isolating which component is the cause.

## Symptoms

- Engine won't crank at all (dead silence when turning key)
- Single click when turning key (starter solenoid engaging but motor not spinning)
- Rapid clicking when turning key (battery too low to hold starter voltage)
- Engine cranks slowly — weak, laboured rotation
- Engine starts fine when warm but struggles after sitting overnight
- Battery warning light on dashboard while driving
- Headlights dim at idle, brighten when revved

## Tips

- Start with a voltage test: battery at rest should be 12.5–12.7V fully charged. Below 12.0V means the battery is discharged or failing.
- With engine running, measure battery terminals. 13.2–14.4V = alternator charging. Below 13.0V = alternator fault or high electrical load.
- Jump-starting solves a flat battery but not a failing alternator — if the vehicle dies again after being jump-started, the alternator is the fault.
- Clicking or no-crank with a known-good battery points to the starter motor or its solenoid.
- Corroded battery terminals cause 20–30% of apparent battery failures. Clean them before buying a new battery.
- Cold weather reduces battery capacity by 30–50%. A battery that works fine in summer may fail its first cold morning.
- A battery older than 4 years in a hot climate should be load-tested, not just voltage-tested — voltage can look normal but capacity is gone.
