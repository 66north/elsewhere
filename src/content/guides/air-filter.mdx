One of the quickest maintenance tasks on the Pajero IV. A clogged air filter restricts airflow, reduces power, and increases fuel consumption — especially on the 4M41 diesel where the turbo amplifies the effect of restriction. Mitsubishi recommends inspection every 15,000 km and replacement every 30,000 km, or more frequently in dusty conditions (off-road, dirt roads).

## Symptoms

- Routine maintenance interval reached (30,000 km)
- Noticeable drop in performance or throttle response
- Increased fuel consumption
- Black smoke on acceleration (diesel)
- Filter visibly grey/brown with debris when inspected

## Tools needed

- Flat-head screwdriver (airbox clips)
- Soft brush or compressed air (to clean airbox housing)

## Tips

- Inspect the old filter by holding it up to light — if you can't see light through it, replace it.
- Take the opportunity to wipe out the inside of the airbox before fitting the new filter.
- Off-road drivers should inspect and replace more frequently — dusty conditions load filters quickly.
- Performance aftermarket filters (K&N, BMC) are available as a reusable alternative.
