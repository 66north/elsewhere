Common torque specifications for the 2008–2013 Pajero IV. Values are from the Mitsubishi factory service manual and apply to both 4M41 diesel and 6G75 petrol engines unless noted otherwise. Always use a calibrated torque wrench on safety-critical fasteners (wheels, brakes, suspension). Do not use air tools to final-torque wheel nuts.

## Tips

- Torque specifications assume clean, dry threads unless the step specifies thread locker or lubricant.
- Always use a calibrated torque wrench — click-type or digital. Beam wrenches are accurate but hard to use in tight spaces.
- Suspension fasteners (arms, struts, anti-roll bars) must be torqued with the vehicle at ride height, not with the suspension hanging. Torquing in the air pre-loads the bushings in a distorted position.
- Wheel nuts: tighten in a star pattern across the wheel, not in a circle. This ensures even seating against the hub.
- If a fastener feels like it reaches torque before the expected angle, stop and inspect — the thread may be stripping or a washer may be missing.
