The Pajero IV wiper system is controlled by the combination switch on the steering column and a wiper relay in the engine bay fuse box. The most common faults are a blown fuse, a failed wiper relay, a seized wiper motor, or a failed wiper link assembly. Intermittent failure often points to the relay or a loose earth connection. Complete failure on all speeds usually means the fuse or relay, not the motor.

## Symptoms

- Wipers do not operate on any speed (check fuse and relay first)
- Wipers operate at HI and LO but not on intermittent — combination switch fault
- Wipers do not park at the rest position and stop mid-sweep — park switch in motor
- Rear wiper not working but front works — separate fuse and motor
- Washer pump not working — separate pump fuse, pump blocked or failed
- Rain sensor wipers not responding — sensor adaptation required after windscreen replacement

## Tools needed

- Multimeter (voltage and continuity)
- Fuse puller

## Tips

- Check the wiper fuse first — it is in the engine bay fuse box, labelled WIPER or FRONT WIPER. The rear wiper is on a separate fuse labelled REAR WIPER.
- To test the relay: pull it out and swap it with an identical relay from the same fuse box (horn relay or similar). If wipers now work, the relay is the fault.
- If the wipers stop in the middle of the windscreen: the park switch inside the motor is faulty. The wipers should always return to the parked position even after the stalk is switched off.
- Rear wiper failure: check the rubber-sealed connector in the tailgate hinge area first. This connector flexes every time the tailgate opens and the contacts corrode. Clean with electrical contact cleaner.
- Washer pump not spraying: check that the washer bottle is full. Then disconnect the hose at the pump and blow through it — a blocked jet nozzle causes apparent pump failure.
- If wipers work on LO/HI but not INT: the intermittent wiper function is part of the combination switch. Replace the switch rather than diagnosing the internal contacts.
- Rain sensor (auto wiper) adaptation: after windscreen replacement, the rain sensor must be adapted to the new glass using a scan tool or by performing the adaptation procedure in the factory manual.
