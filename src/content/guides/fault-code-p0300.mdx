Code P0300 indicates the ECU has detected random misfires across multiple cylinders. A misfire occurs when one or more cylinders fail to ignite the fuel-air mixture, causing power loss, rough idle, and hesitation. Unlike P0301-P0306 (which point to a specific cylinder), P0300 means the problem affects multiple cylinders randomly. Common causes are weak spark, fuel delivery issues, or air leaks.

## Symptoms

- Check Engine light illuminated
- Rough idle (noticeable vibration)
- Hesitation or stumbling during acceleration
- Loss of power, especially under load
- Black smoke from exhaust (overfueling)
- Fuel smell from exhaust
- Poor fuel economy
- Engine may run and then stall

## Tips

- Start with spark plug replacement — it's the most common cause and cheapest fix.
- If P0300 appears after refueling at an unfamiliar station, suspect fuel contamination. Drain tank and refill with quality fuel from a trusted brand.
- Vacuum leaks (cracked hoses, loose fittings) are the second most common cause. Spray soapy water to find them — bubbles reveal the leak.
- Do NOT ignore this code. Continued driving with misfires damages the catalytic converter (very expensive).
- If misfire codes appear only on cold mornings, suspect failing glow plugs (4M41 diesel) or old battery (weak cold start).
- Water in fuel is common in humid climates or after driving through flooded roads. A fuel dryer additive may help; if not, drain tank.
