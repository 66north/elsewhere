The Pajero IV has front CV (constant velocity) joints and rear propshaft universal joints. The front CV joints allow the wheels to steer while transmitting drive. They are packed with grease inside rubber boots — when a boot tears, grease escapes, grit enters, and the joint destroys itself within weeks. Inspect boots at every 20,000 km service. Clicking or clunking when turning sharply at low speed is a classic sign of a worn outer CV joint.

## Symptoms

- Clicking or clunking sound when turning sharply at low speed (outer CV joint)
- Vibration through steering wheel or floor at highway speed (front driveshaft balance)
- Grease flung onto wheel or inner guard (torn CV boot)
- Vibration through floor increasing with speed (rear propshaft U-joint)
- Knocking from underneath during acceleration from rest (worn inner CV or U-joint)
- Torn rubber boot visible on front driveshaft

## Tools needed

- 17mm socket (wheel bolts)
- 30mm socket or equivalent (hub nut — if removing driveshaft)
- CV boot clamp pliers
- CV joint grease (molybdenum disulphide type)
- Torque wrench

## Tips

- Inspect CV boots at every oil change — crouch beside the front wheel and look at the driveshaft for grease spray or visible boot damage.
- A torn boot with no clicking yet means the joint is still good — replace the boot and regrease before the joint wears. This is a $30 fix vs $150+ for a new joint.
- Clicking on full lock in one direction only indicates the outer CV joint on that side. Clicking in both directions usually means an inner joint.
- Do not confuse CV joint clicking with brake pad wear indicator squeal — brake squeal is continuous at low speed, CV click is only when turning.
- Off-road driving over rocks and obstacles tears CV boots rapidly. Check them after every off-road trip.
