Quick reference for dashboard warning lights on the Pajero IV. Red lights require immediate attention — stop safely as soon as possible. Amber lights indicate a fault that needs diagnosis but is not immediately dangerous. Blue and green lights are informational. When in doubt, connect an OBD-II scanner to read the fault code before driving further.

## Symptoms

- Warning light illuminated on dashboard
- Multiple warning lights on after startup (may be a battery or sensor fault)
- Warning light on but vehicle appears to drive normally

## Tips

- RED warning lights: stop the vehicle as soon as it is safe. Oil pressure, engine temperature, and brake warning lights indicate conditions that cause immediate engine or safety damage.
- AMBER warning lights: continue driving cautiously to a workshop. Check Engine, ABS, traction control, and suspension lights should be scanned before next long journey.
- All lights illuminating at startup and then going off is normal — this is a bulb check. Lights staying on after 3 seconds indicate a fault.
- Check Engine light (MIL): connect an OBD-II scanner to read the fault code. The light alone tells you nothing — the code tells you everything.
- ABS light on without Check Engine: usually a wheel speed sensor fault. Safe to drive but ABS is disabled.
- Battery light on while driving: alternator is not charging. Drive directly to a workshop — the battery will go flat within 20–40 minutes.
- Oil pressure light on while driving: stop immediately, do not restart. Check oil level. If oil level is correct, the oil pump or pressure sensor may have failed.
- Glow plug light (diesel) staying on or flashing: indicates a glow plug circuit fault. Cold starting will be affected. See P0380 fault code guide.
- DPF light (diesel): exhaust filter needs regeneration. Take a 30-minute motorway drive immediately. If light remains, workshop regeneration required.
- TPMS light (tyre pressure): check all four tyre pressures including spare. Light resets when pressures are corrected and vehicle is driven above 25 km/h.
