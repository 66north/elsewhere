Engine coolant degrades over time as additives break down and corrosion products accumulate. Factory intervals call for a complete coolant flush every 40,000 km or every 2 years, whichever comes first. Neglecting this allows scale buildup in the radiator, water jacket, and heater core, reducing cooling efficiency and promoting rust inside the engine block. Both 4M41 diesel and 6G75 petrol use the same coolant specification.

## Symptoms

- Scheduled maintenance interval (40,000 km or 2 years)
- [object Object]
- Overheating or slow warm-up during winter
- Sweet smell from the exhaust (coolant leak into combustion chamber) or from engine bay
- Visible corrosion or white crusty deposits on radiator fins or hoses
- Heater not blowing hot air despite engine temperature being normal

## Tools needed

- Pan or drain container (minimum 10 liters capacity)
- Screwdriver or wrench for radiator drain plug (usually Phillips head or 10mm hex)
- Large funnel with a filter or cloth strainer
- Soft hose or siphon (to drain hard-to-reach areas if needed)
- Coolant mixer or distilled water (to dilute concentrate if using concentrate)
- Thermometer (optional, to verify final coolant temperature)

## Tips

- Park the vehicle on level ground and allow the engine to cool completely (at least 1 hour) before starting work. Hot coolant can scald.
- The radiator drain plug is on the bottom driver's side of the radiator. Locate it before draining; it may be hidden behind a splash guard.
- Drain the radiator first (10 minutes), then drain the coolant reservoir if accessible (held by a clip, pops off easily).
- After draining, you may need to remove the lower radiator hose and squeeze its contents into the drain pan.
- [object Object]
- Refill with 50/50 premixed coolant (or concentrate + distilled water mixed in equal parts). Do not use tap water, which contains minerals that cause scale.
- Run the engine with the radiator cap off for 5–10 minutes to purge air bubbles from the system. You will see bubbles rising. Top up as needed.
- Reinstall the radiator cap only after the engine has cooled completely.
- Check the coolant level again after 100 km of driving. Thermal expansion will draw in more coolant if needed.
- Some vehicles have a bleed screw on the thermostat housing (top of engine). If the system is hard to fill (lots of air), open this screw briefly while filling to release trapped air.
