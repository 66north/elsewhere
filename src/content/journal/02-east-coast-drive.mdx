The east coast of Iceland is often overlooked by tourists rushing toward the Ring Road. It's worth the detour. Fewer cars, better roads, and a rugged beauty that feels less curated than the south coast.

## Route 1 from Egilsstaðir

Starting from Egilsstaðir, Route 1 hugs the coastline. The road is excellent—wide, well-maintained, free of the gravel and washboard that characterizes the north. We pushed south toward Höfn at a steady pace.

## Waterfall Country

Fjaðrármúli, Skaftafell, Svartifoss—each valley seems to have its own waterfall. We stopped frequently to explore. The Pajero's turning radius and low ground clearance made it easier to park near trailheads than larger SUVs. One morning, we hiked to Svartifoss through hiking-boot country; the Pajero sat in the parking area, a reminder that some places are meant to be reached on foot.

## Vatnajökull & the South

As we approached Höfn, Vatnajökull dominated the horizon. The glacier's ice tongues—Breiðamerkurjökull, Skaftafellsjökull—descend into the valleys like frozen rivers. We took the glacier tour on foot; the Pajero watched from the car park.

**Fuel consumption:** 8.2 L/100km on Highway 1. The engine runs efficiently at steady cruising speeds.

**Next stop:** We're aiming for Húsavík by week's end. Pushing north through the interior again.
