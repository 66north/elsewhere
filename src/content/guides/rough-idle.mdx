A rough or shaky idle at a stoplight or in neutral indicates an imbalance in combustion. The 4M41 diesel should idle smoothly at 650-750 RPM; the 6G75 petrol at 600-800 RPM. If the engine vibrates, stutters, or threatens to stall, a systematic diagnosis can pinpoint whether the issue is ignition, fuel delivery, air intake, or mechanical wear. Most rough idle fixes are inexpensive (spark plugs, fuel injector cleaning) and can prevent more serious damage.

## Symptoms

- Engine shakes or vibrates noticeably at idle (felt through steering wheel or seat)
- RPM fluctuates (bounces between 500-900 RPM instead of steady)
- Engine sounds like it might stall but catches itself
- Rough idle disappears when accelerating (suggests fuel trim or cylinder imbalance)
- Check Engine light illuminated (P0300 or P0301-P0306 misfire codes)
- White or black smoke from exhaust when idling (bad combustion)
- Hesitation when shifting into Drive (load aggravates the condition)

## Tools needed

- OBD2 scan tool to read fault codes
- Fuel pressure gauge (optional, for fuel system checks)
- Spark plug gap tool (6G75 petrol)
- Soapy water spray (to find vacuum leaks)
- Compression gauge (optional, to diagnose mechanical wear)

## Tips

- Rough idle is rarely dangerous, but ignoring it can lead to worse misfires and poor fuel economy
- If rough idle is intermittent (comes and goes), suspect a failing sensor (MAF, O2) rather than worn plugs
- [object Object]
- If rough idle persists after spark plug replacement (6G75), suspect coil pack or fuel injector, not the plugs
- Some rough idle can be caused by a failing transmission torque converter (6G75 auto). The converter allows the engine to load differently at idle
- Diesel engines (4M41) often have slight vibration at idle due to the combustion process. Some roughness is normal; compare to a known-good vehicle
