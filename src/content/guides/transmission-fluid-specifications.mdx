The Pajero IV uses a 5-speed automatic transmission (Aisin) that relies on transmission fluid for lubrication, cooling, and pressure. Degraded or incorrect fluid causes slipping, delayed shifts, and eventual transmission failure. This guide covers ATF type, capacity, and service intervals for your 5-speed automatic transmission.

## Tips

- Check transmission fluid level when the engine is warm and transmission is in Park. The dipstick has separate marks for warm checks.
- Overfilled transmission fluid causes foaming, overheating, and poor shift quality. Never exceed the MAX mark on the dipstick.
- Transmission fluid loses its friction modifiers over time. Even if the level is correct, old fluid causes poor shifts and slipping.
- If your transmission starts slipping or shifting roughly, change the fluid and filter immediately. A clogged filter restricts pressure and damages the transmission.
- Transmission fluid absorbs moisture (hygroscopic). Leaving the cap off or driving through water can introduce water into the fluid, causing corrosion.
- If transmission fluid is black or smells burnt, the transmission is overheating. Check for: - Radiator or transmission cooler blockage - Damaged fan or thermostat - Worn internal friction material (requires transmission overhaul)
- Never use power steering fluid or engine oil in the transmission — it will cause immediate failure.
