The Faroe Islands offer some of the most dramatic landscapes accessible by vehicle in the North Atlantic. Narrow roads wind through valleys with vertical cliffs, waterfalls, and Puffin colonies.

## 2025 Trip Summary
Spent 10 days exploring the 18 islands. Despite the small size, there's enough terrain and road variety to keep driving interesting. The Pajero's turning radius was tested many times on single-track roads with passing places.

## Highlights
- **Sørvágsvatn Lake**: 30m cliffs with ocean 150m away, optical illusion of water meeting ocean
- **Dimmuborgir**: Black lava formations near Streymoy
- **Kallur Lighthouse Trail**: Switchbacks accessed only by foot

## Vehicle Condition
- Ferry loading was straightforward with good ramps
- All roads passable with standard tires (not required)
- No damage despite constant tight roads
