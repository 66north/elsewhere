Uneven tire wear (inner or outer edge worn faster than center) is a warning sign of alignment problems, suspension wear, or tire pressure issues. While tires are a normal wear item, uneven wear shortens their lifespan and increases replacement cost. Early diagnosis and correction can extend tire life by 20,000-30,000 km. This guide covers diagnosing the cause and correcting alignment.

## Symptoms

- Inner or outer edges of tires worn significantly more than the center
- Scalloping or cup-wear pattern (dips around the tire circumference indicate suspension bounce)
- One tire worn much faster than others (possible alignment issue affecting one axle)
- Vibration or shimmy in the steering wheel at highway speed (tire imbalance or worn suspension)
- Vehicle pulls to one side when braking (possible brake caliper sticking, not alignment)
- Sidewall bulges or flat spots (tire damage or extreme imbalance)
- Excessive tire noise or humming at highway speeds

## Tools needed

- Tire pressure gauge (analog or digital)
- Tread depth gauge (or penny test)
- Wheel alignment shop equipment (use a professional, not DIY)
- Jack and jack stands (to lift tire for inspection)

## Tips

- Wheel alignment drifts gradually. Get aligned every 20,000 km or if you notice uneven wear or pulling
- Tire pressure changes seasonally. Check pressure more often in winter (cold air contracts) and summer (heat expands)"
- Worn suspension (shocks, bushings, ball joints) affects alignment. Before spending money on alignment, have suspension inspected"
- [object Object]
- Some uneven wear is normal in high-performance driving or aggressive cornering. Minimize hard cornering to extend tire life"
