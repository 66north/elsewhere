Coolant prevents your engine from overheating and protects against corrosion and freezing. The Pajero IV cooling system holds approximately 9.5 liters and requires a specific type of coolant compatible with Mitsubishi's aluminum engine blocks and radiators. Using the wrong coolant can cause corrosion, leaks, and engine damage. This guide covers coolant selection, capacity, and flush intervals for both the 4M41 diesel and 6G75 petrol engines.

## Tips

- Always let the engine cool before opening the radiator cap — pressurized coolant can cause severe burns.
- Check coolant level in the expansion tank (not the radiator directly). Top up with the same type if low.
- Never use water alone as coolant — it freezes in winter and lacks corrosion inhibitors.
- A 50/50 mix of coolant and distilled water is standard for most climates. In very cold regions (-30°C or below), use 60% coolant / 40% water.
- If your coolant level drops frequently, you have a leak. Do not ignore this — a running engine without coolant overheats in seconds.
- White crusty deposits on the engine or radiator indicate coolant leaks or a failed gasket. Have the system inspected.
- When flushing, use distilled water to rinse the system, not tap water. Tap water contains minerals that corrode.
