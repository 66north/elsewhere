Everyone drives Route 1's south coast. In summer, it's crowded with rental cars. In winter, it's a gauntlet of ice, wind, and beauty that separates the committed from the casual.

## Vík & Reynisfjara

We started at Vík, where the black sand beach and basalt columns meet a violent Atlantic. Wind gusted to 60+ km/h. The Pajero sat steady on its suspension while lighter vehicles rocked in the gusts.

Reynisfjara beach is iconic—for good reason. Waves crash over the basalt, seabirds circle, and the landscape feels alien. Winter visits fewer tourists, which means better light and fewer crowds. We had the beach almost to ourselves.

## Skaftafell & the Glaciers

Pushing east on Route 1, we entered glacier country. Skaftafellsjökull descended into its valley, pristine and vast. The tour operators were running their last trips before winter closures; we declined and explored the hiking trails on our own.

A full day from Vík to Jökulsárlón took 5 hours with photo stops. Route 1 is excellent here—no surprises, good visibility, manageable.

## Jökulsárlón

The glacier lagoon in winter is surreal. Fewer tours, calmer conditions, and a particular quality of light—low angle in February, turning the ice blue and gold. We watched a calving event: a small iceberg the size of a house broke from the glacier and splashed into the lagoon.

**Fuel:** 7.8 L/100km on the highway, 8.2 L/100km accounting for exploration driving. Managed the tank well between Vik and Höfn.

**Route assessment:** South coast in winter is safe if you drive carefully. The Pajero's all-terrain tires gave confidence even on ice-glazed sections.
