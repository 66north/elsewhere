The Pajero IV INVECS-III 5-speed automatic transmission has an internal filter (strainer) inside the transmission pan. Mitsubishi does not publish a filter replacement interval — it is treated as part of a fluid change at major overhauls. In practice, replacing the filter with every second or third fluid change (every 80,000–120,000 km) is good preventive maintenance, particularly if the pan drops show metallic debris or dark sludge. The filter is accessed by removing the transmission pan.

## Symptoms

- Routine transmission service or high-mileage preventive maintenance
- Metallic debris on drain plug magnet when dropping the pan
- Sluggish or delayed gear engagement, especially cold
- Transmission shudder during torque converter lock-up (4th/5th gear cruise)

## Tools needed

- 10mm socket (transmission pan bolts)
- Drain pan (minimum 5L)
- Magnet (to collect debris from pan)
- Torque wrench

## Tips

- The transmission drain plug (if present) drains only the pan — drop the pan to access the filter and inspect for debris.
- Inspect the pan magnet carefully: fine grey metallic fuzz is normal bearing and clutch wear; larger particles or flakes indicate internal damage requiring further investigation.
- The strainer is press-fit into the valve body. Prise it off gently — do not use excessive force. Confirm the O-ring comes out with the old filter, not left in the housing.
- Clean the pan with solvent before reinstalling — old sludge reduces the capacity of the new filter immediately.
- Refill with ATF SP-III only. Check fluid level with the engine warm at operating temperature, selector in Park, on a level surface.
- After refilling, cycle through all gear positions (P-R-N-D-3-2-1-P) to fill the valve body before checking the final level.
