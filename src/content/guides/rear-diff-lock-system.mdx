The Pajero's locking rear differential (rear diff lock or RDL) mechanically locks the left and right rear axles together for maximum traction in extreme off-road conditions. When locked, both rear wheels turn at the same speed regardless of grip, eliminating the open-differential tendency to spin the wheel with the least traction. The system changed across generations — Gen 2/3 use a vacuum-actuated system, while Gen 4 integrates Active Stability Control (ATSC) management. Knowing how it works and common failure points helps diagnose a diff lock that won't engage or disengage.

## Symptoms

- Diff lock warning lamp flashes but never goes solid (engagement failure)
- Diff lock lamp stays on after disengaging (stuck engaged)
- No response when diff lock button is pressed
- Grinding or clunking when attempting to engage or disengage at speed
- Vehicle pulling to one side on a straight paved road (diff lock engaged by mistake)

## Tips

- On pavement with the diff lock accidentally engaged, you will feel tight steering and may hear tyre scrub in turns — disengage immediately.
- Always disengage the rear diff lock before driving on paved roads or at speed. Driving with it locked on pavement causes severe drivetrain stress.
- If the diff lock won't disengage on a slope, try rocking the vehicle forwards and backwards while operating the switch.
- Gen 2/3 vacuum lines are often the culprit after 20+ years — budget for a full vacuum hose replacement if the system is acting erratically.
