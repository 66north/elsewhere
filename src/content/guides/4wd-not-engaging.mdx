The Pajero IV uses a Super Select 4WD system (SS4-II) with four modes: 2H (rear-wheel drive), 4H (part-time 4WD high), 4HLc (4WD high with centre diff locked), and 4LLc (4WD low with centre diff locked). Engagement problems are usually electrical (4WD selector switch, transfer case motor, or wiring) rather than mechanical. The 4WD indicator light on the dashboard confirms the system status — a flashing light indicates an incomplete shift.

## Symptoms

- 4WD indicator light flashing or not illuminating after selector switch change
- Transfer case makes grinding or clicking during mode change
- Vehicle not responding as expected in 4WD (understeer suggests 2H only)
- 4WD engages but low range won't engage (or vice versa)
- Selector dial turns but no mode change occurs
- 4WD light on permanently — system stuck in one mode

## Tips

- For 2H→4H shifts: vehicle must be below 100 km/h. The shift occurs within 1–2 seconds with a light click. If the indicator flashes, the shift is incomplete — stop, select 2H, and try again.
- For 4H→4LLc: stop the vehicle, select Neutral, then move to 4LLc. Never shift to low range while moving.
- A flashing 4WD indicator means the transfer case actuator motor is running but has not completed the shift. Most common causes: speed too high, tyres spinning, or actuator motor fault.
- Front differential engagement on models with locking front diff: the front diff lock indicator should illuminate within 3–5 seconds. Delay usually means the actuator motor is slow or its connector is corroded.
- If 4WD was never used for a long time, the transfer case may be stiff. Engage and disengage several times while driving slowly — this frees up the actuator.
- Check the transfer case fluid — low or degraded fluid causes actuator motor strain and slow shifts.
- The 4WD system uses the ABS wheel speed sensors to detect speed conditions for engagement. ABS faults can prevent 4WD engagement.
