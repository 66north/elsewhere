Power window faults on the Pajero IV are usually a blown fuse, a failed window switch, a worn motor, or a broken regulator cable. The driver's door master switch controls all windows and has a window lock button that disables the passenger switches — check this first. Individual window failures point to the motor or switch for that door; all-window failures point to the main fuse.

## Symptoms

- One window not responding to switch (motor or switch fault)
- All windows stopped working (fuse or master switch fault)
- Window moves slowly or stops midway (worn motor or jammed regulator)
- Window works from master switch but not from door switch (door switch fault)
- Grinding or clicking noise when operating window
- Window drops into door and won't rise (regulator cable snapped)

## Tips

- Check the window lock button on the driver's master switch — pressing it disables all passenger window switches. This is mistaken for a fault regularly.
- Check fuse 19 (15A) in the interior fuse box for the power window circuit. A blown fuse stops all windows.
- To test if the motor is the fault: apply 12V directly to the motor connector. If it runs, the switch or wiring is at fault. If it does not run, the motor is dead.
- Window regulator cables fray and snap on older vehicles, particularly in humid climates. The window will drop freely into the door when the cable breaks.
- Window switch contacts corrode on doors that are frequently exposed to water ingress. Cleaning the switch contacts with contact cleaner often restores function.
- If a window works only intermittently and gets stuck when hot, the motor brushes are worn — it works when cold but fails when the motor heats up.
