The V5AWF 5-speed automatic transmission fitted to the Gen 3 and Gen 4 Pajero (both diesel 4M41 and petrol 6G75 variants) uses an overflow-plug fill system — fluid is added until it flows out of a separate overflow hole at the correct level. There is no conventional dipstick. The drain-and-fill procedure replaces approximately 3 litres of fluid per pass (roughly half the total capacity), so multiple passes are needed for a full flush. Always check the fluid level after any fill using the ATF temperature-check procedure to confirm correct fill.

## Symptoms

- Transmission slipping or jerky gear changes (low or degraded fluid)
- Dark, discoloured, or burnt-smelling ATF on the filler plug dipstick
- Gearbox warning light or fault codes related to fluid temperature
- High-mileage service (40,000–60,000 km intervals recommended)

## Tools needed

- Drain tray (minimum 4 L capacity)
- Funnel and extension hose to reach the filler plug
- Socket set — filler, overflow, and drain plugs are typically 24 mm or similar
- Torque wrench
- Rag and protective sheet (fluid will drip onto centre member — place a protective sheet)

## Tips

- A single drain-and-fill only replaces about 50–60% of the total fluid — the rest remains in the torque converter. For a thorough fluid change, do two passes (drain, fill, drive 10 km, drain again, fill).
- The overflow-plug fill system ensures the correct fluid level at the correct temperature — do not overfill by adding extra fluid above the overflow point.
- If fluid did not drip from the overflow hole after refilling, the level is too low. Do not drive — add more fluid until overflow drips occur.
- Keep the protective sheet under the centre member — ATF is a skin and paint irritant and is very slippery on concrete.
- Some Pajero variants use a fluid with a specific colour (red ATF SP-III) — if the drained fluid is brown-black, it has been in service too long.
- The factory recommends ATF SP-III. Some owners use Dexron VI (backwards compatible with III), but verify compatibility with Mitsubishi specifications for your year.
