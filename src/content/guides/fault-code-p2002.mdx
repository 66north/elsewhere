P2002 means the ECU has detected that the Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) is not performing regeneration correctly, or the DPF is so clogged that it cannot be regenerated. The DPF traps soot particles and periodically burns them off during highway driving (passive regeneration) or via an active injection cycle. This code commonly appears on vehicles used predominantly for short urban trips, which prevent the exhaust from reaching regeneration temperature.

## Symptoms

- DPF warning light illuminated (amber lamp)
- Check Engine light illuminated
- Noticeable loss of power (limp mode in severe cases)
- Increased fuel consumption
- Oily residue from exhaust tip
- White or grey smoke at startup or under load

## Tips

- Use only ACEA C3 rated engine oil in the 4M41 with DPF. Standard ACEA A3/B4 oil contains higher ash content that cannot be burned off and permanently blocks the DPF.
- At least one monthly motorway drive at 60+ km/h for 30+ minutes is essential for DPF health. Urban-only driving will block the DPF within 50,000 km.
- Check engine oil level if P2002 appears — rising oil level is a sign of post-injection fuel contamination, which damages the engine and DPF simultaneously.
- Do NOT attempt to remove or bypass the DPF — this is illegal in most markets and triggers additional fault codes affecting engine management.
- If the vehicle is exclusively used in urban driving, consider professional DPF cleaning every 60,000–80,000 km as preventive maintenance.
- A forced regeneration should not be performed in an enclosed space — the exhaust temperature rises to 600°C+ during active regen.
