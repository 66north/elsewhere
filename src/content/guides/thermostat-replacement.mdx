The thermostat controls coolant flow to maintain the engine at operating temperature (~88°C). A stuck-open thermostat causes P0128 (engine runs cold), poor fuel economy, and a heater that never gets hot. A stuck-closed thermostat causes overheating. Replacement requires a partial coolant drain and takes about an hour. Always use a new gasket and refill with the correct coolant.

## Symptoms

- Temperature gauge stuck low or slow to reach midpoint (stuck open)
- Overheating — gauge climbing above midpoint (stuck closed)
- P0128 fault code stored
- Heater not producing warm air even when engine is warm
- Poor fuel economy (cold engine runs richer)

## Tools needed

- 10mm socket and ratchet (thermostat housing bolts)
- Flat-head screwdriver (hose clamps)
- Drain pan (minimum 3L)
- Torque wrench

## Tips

- Drain only 2–3 litres of coolant from the lower radiator hose — you do not need to fully drain the system.
- Clean the thermostat housing sealing surface before fitting the new gasket. Aluminium surfaces corrode and leave bumps that cause leaks.
- After refilling, run the engine with the heater on full and the cap off the expansion tank. Squeeze the upper radiator hose to help purge air bubbles.
- A stuck-open thermostat is much more common than a stuck-closed one — the spring mechanism wears open, not shut.
- If the temperature gauge reads normal but P0128 is stored, the coolant temperature sensor may be the actual fault — check sensor resistance before replacing the thermostat.
