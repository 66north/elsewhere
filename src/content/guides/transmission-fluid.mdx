All Pajero Gen IV models (2008–2013) come with 5-speed automatic transmission. The transmission fluid (ATF) serves as coolant, lubricant, and hydraulic medium for valve actuation. Over 60,000 km, the fluid oxidizes, additives break down, and friction modifiers deteriorate, reducing shift quality and transmission efficiency. Factory service intervals call for fluid changes every 60,000 km or earlier if the vehicle is used for towing or heavy city driving.

## Symptoms

- Scheduled maintenance interval (60,000 km, or 40,000 km if towing)
- Transmission fluid appears dark brown or smells burnt (indicates overheating or oxidation)
- Sluggish or delayed shift response (transmission hesitates 1–2 seconds before engaging gears)
- Shuddering during acceleration (worn friction material in torque converter or fluid breakdown)
- [object Object]
- Leaking red fluid under the vehicle (ATF is bright red when new, darkens with age)

## Tools needed

- Transmission pan drain wrench or socket (usually 17mm hex)
- Pan or drain container with 10+ liters capacity
- Gasket scraper or plastic scraper to remove old pan gasket material without scratching
- Rags and shop towels in large quantity (ATF is messy)
- Hand pump or electric oil pump to refill transmission (much faster than a funnel)
- Transmission fluid dipstick to check final level (already in vehicle)

## Tips

- Perform this service when the transmission is warm (idle the engine for 5 minutes before draining). Warm fluid flows faster and carries more contaminants out
- The transmission pan is mounted under the vehicle, below the transmission. You will need to raise the vehicle on a lift or jack stands to access it safely
- Drain the pan slowly — ATF is hot and can cause burns. Position the drain pan directly below the drain plug
- Let the pan drain for at least 15 minutes. Some residual fluid will remain in the pan and torque converter (maybe 2 liters), so expect the system to need less than full capacity for a complete refill
- [object Object]
- Scrape the pan sealing surface clean of old gasket material. Use a plastic scraper to avoid scratching the aluminum surface
- [object Object]
- Reinstall the pan with a new gasket, tightening bolts in a cross pattern (alternating opposite sides) to ensure even seating
- Refill the transmission with ATF to the "COLD" mark on the dipstick if the transmission is at room temperature, or "HOT" if the transmission is still warm
- After refilling, start the engine and move the shift lever through all gears (P-R-N-D) several times while holding the brake. This circulates the new fluid through the torque converter
- Recheck the dipstick after this procedure. The level may have changed as air bubbles purge from the system
- Drive the vehicle gently for 10–15 km, then recheck the transmission temperature and fluid level. A proper fluid change improves shift quality noticeably
