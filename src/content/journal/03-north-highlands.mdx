February in North Iceland is harsh. Most F-roads are locked behind snow gates. But the alternative routes—unpaved but drivable—open up a different kind of adventure. Fewer tourists. More silence.

## Akureyri to the Interior

We left Akureyri early, fuel tanks full, extra food and a satellite communicator. Route 1 was dry until we turned inland toward Blönduós. From there, Route 68 climbs into the highlands.

The road deteriorated gradually—asphalt to gravel to washboard to talcum-powder dust. The Pajero's suspension absorbed it all. At this point in the trip, the drivetrain feels tighter than when we started 17,000 km ago.

## The Highlands at Winter

There's a peculiar beauty to empty landscapes. No cars. No tourists. Just the sound of wind and the occasional ptarmigan. We camped in a sheltered valley near Tjörnes, where geothermal heat keeps a small area ice-free even in winter.

## Mechanical Notes

The 4M40 diesel runs smoothly at altitude, where petrol engines gasp. We used extra fuel heating the cab at night—roughly 2L per night with the cabin heater running moderately. Manageable.

**Tire performance:** The Bridgestones are performing well even at 248,310 km. Tread depth is borderline for another winter, though. Planning a tire service in Akureyri before the next expedition.

**What's next:** Heading south toward Reykjavík for scheduled maintenance and resupply. Route back to Húsavík to pick up some expedition members.
