The V5AWF automatic transmission has no dipstick — fluid level is checked through an overflow-plug system and is critically temperature-dependent. The check must be performed with fluid at exactly 39–46°C. The transmission has a built-in ATF temperature warning lamp (on the instrument cluster) that tells you when you are in the correct temperature window. Checking at the wrong temperature gives a false reading. This procedure explains how to warm the transmission and verify the level without a scan tool.

## Tools needed

- Wire or paperclip to ground OBD diagnosis connector terminal No. 1
- Socket set for overflow and filler plugs
- Torque wrench
- New overflow plug gasket (always replace)
- New filler plug O-ring (always replace)
- ATF to top up if needed

## Tips

- If checking in warm weather (ambient above 20°C), the ATF may reach temperature faster — watch the warning lamp instead of relying solely on time.
- The ATF temperature warning lamp is not a permanent fixture on all Pajero instrument clusters — check your specific year. If the lamp is absent, use a transmission temperature reader (OBD adapter with live data) to verify temperature.
- The N → D → N cycling trick warms the converter faster than just idling — it forces fluid circulation through all hydraulic circuits.
- Always use new sealing washers/O-rings when removing the overflow and filler plugs, even if just checking the level.
- If topping up, use only the specified ATF (SP-III or equivalent). Mixing incompatible fluids can cause clutch pack damage.
