Your Pajero IV's tires and wheels are engineered for load capacity, off-road capability, and comfort. Using the wrong size, load rating, or pressure reduces handling, increases fuel consumption, and can damage the suspension. This guide covers factory tire sizes, wheel specifications, and pressure recommendations for your Pajero IV.

## Tips

- Tire pressure drops 1 PSI for every 10°C temperature decrease. Check pressure monthly, especially in winter.
- Replacing one tire on a 4WD vehicle can damage the differential — always replace all four if possible, or have the new tire shaved to match the others.
- All-terrain or mud-terrain tires are heavier and drag more than highway tires, reducing fuel economy by 10-15%.
- Wheel balancing should be done every 10,000 km or when you notice vibration at highway speed.
- When rotating tires, inspect brakes, suspension components, and wheel bearings for wear.
- Nitrogen-filled tires (common on new vehicles) retain pressure better than air, but regular air is acceptable if you check pressure monthly.
- Running on a flat tire or severely under-inflated tire damages the sidewall. Repair is not possible; tire must be replaced.
