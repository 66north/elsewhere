The alternator charges the battery while the engine runs. A failing alternator causes the battery to drain, leading to a no-start condition that is often misdiagnosed as a battery fault. Test the alternator before replacing the battery. Output should be 13.2–14.4V at the battery terminals with the engine running. Replacement requires removing the serpentine belt and is a 1–2 hour job.

## Symptoms

- Battery warning light on dashboard (red battery icon)
- Battery repeatedly going flat despite being new
- Dimming headlights at idle that brighten when engine is revved
- Whining noise from engine bay that changes with RPM
- Electrical accessories (windows, lights) behaving erratically
- Voltmeter reading below 12.5V with engine running

## Tools needed

- Multimeter (DC voltage range)
- Belt tensioner wrench
- 13mm, 14mm sockets
- Torque wrench

## Tips

- Test before replacing: with engine running at idle, battery voltage should be 13.2–14.4V. Rev to 2000 RPM — should climb to 14.0–14.8V.
- If voltage is 12.5–13.0V at idle, the alternator may just have worn brushes — a $20 fix vs $250 for a full unit.
- A whining noise that changes with engine RPM is typically a failing alternator bearing, not brush wear.
- Disconnect the negative battery terminal before removing the alternator — the B+ terminal is live at all times.
- Remanufactured alternators carry a 1–2 year warranty and are 40–60% cheaper than new OEM. Perfectly acceptable for a non-original repair.
