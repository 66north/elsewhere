The 6G75 petrol engine has six spark plugs, one per cylinder. Factory service intervals recommend replacement every 40,000 km for standard copper plugs, or every 100,000 km for premium platinum/iridium plugs (which cost more upfront but last longer). Worn plugs increase fuel consumption, reduce starting reliability, and cause hesitation under acceleration. The job is straightforward — no special tools required beyond a spark plug socket.

## Symptoms

- Scheduled maintenance interval (40,000 km for copper, 100,000 km for premium)
- Difficulty starting, especially in cold weather
- Rough idle or stumbling during acceleration
- Visible spark plug fouling (black soot, oil residue, or gap erosion when inspected)
- Reduced fuel economy or loss of performance
- Slight hesitation when upshifting under light load

## Tools needed

- Spark plug socket (usually 16mm, with rubber insert to protect the ceramic insulator)
- Socket wrench and ratchet
- Spark plug gap tool or feeler gauge (to check gap before installation)
- Wire stripper or coil pack removal tool (if needed to access plugs under coils)
- Clean soft-bristle brush (to clean around spark plug holes)
- Engine cover removal tools (plastic clips, if present)

## Tips

- The spark plugs are located under six individual coil packs on top of the engine (one coil per cylinder). Each coil sits directly on a spark plug.
- Remove one coil at a time by unclipping the connector and twisting the coil counterclockwise. The spark plug sits directly underneath.
- Inspect the spark plug wire or coil boot for cracks, corrosion, or oil residue. Clean if dirty; replace if damaged.
- If a spark plug breaks inside the hole during installation, do not force it. Back out carefully with pliers or a screw extractor.
- Check the gap on new plugs before installation. Most come pre-gapped to 1.0mm, but factory settings can vary.
- [object Object]
- After replacing all six, start the engine and listen for any unusual knocking. The engine may run slightly rough for 1–2 minutes as the ECU adapts to the new plugs.
- If the Check Engine light appears after replacement, scan for misfires (P0300–P0306) and verify coil-to-plug connections are seated firmly.
