All Pajero Gen IV models come with 5-speed automatic transmission. If the transmission slips (engine RPM jumps but vehicle speed doesn't), hesitates before engaging a gear, or shift points feel wrong, the transmission fluid is likely degraded, internal friction material is worn, or solenoid valves are stuck. This is a serious condition that worsens over time and can lead to complete transmission failure if ignored. Early diagnosis and fluid changes can often resolve the issue.

## Symptoms

- Engine RPM rises but vehicle acceleration is delayed (slipping between gears)
- Shift feel is mushy or delayed (1-2 seconds of hesitation before engaging)
- Transmission shifts too early or too late (shift points inconsistent with driving conditions)
- Whining or grinding noise from transmission during acceleration
- [object Object]
- Vehicle will not move or moves very slowly despite engine running (internal failure)
- Check Engine light with transmission-related code (P0870, P0875, etc.)

## Tools needed

- OBD2 scan tool to read transmission codes
- Transmission fluid dipstick (included in vehicle)
- Transmission fluid and filter (see transmission-fluid.yml)
- Thermometer (optional, to measure cooler temperature)

## Tips

- Transmission fluid condition is critical. Even small particles (wear debris) can accumulate and cause solenoid sticking
- Transmission slipping feels different from engine power loss. If RPM jumps but speed doesn't, it's transmission. If both lag, it's engine"
- Some slipping is normal during cold-start before fluid warms. If slipping persists after 5-10 minutes of driving, something is wrong"
- Automatic transmissions have no "neutral" on the highway. Always use P (park) or N (neutral) when stopped; avoid coasting in N"
- If transmission suddenly fails to move the vehicle, stop immediately. Continued driving can damage the transmission beyond repair"
