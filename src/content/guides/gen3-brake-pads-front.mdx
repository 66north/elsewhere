Front brake pad replacement on the Gen 3 Pajero (NM / NP series, 2000–2006) is a straightforward job accessible to any home mechanic. The front brakes use a single sliding caliper on each side with two pads. Always replace pads in axle pairs (both fronts together) and inspect the disc surface for deep scoring or thickness below the minimum. The Gen 3 front caliper is similar to Gen 4 but with minor differences in caliper guide pin dimensions — use Gen 3 specific parts.

## Symptoms

- Squealing or grinding when braking
- Brake warning light (pad wear indicator)
- Reduced braking performance or longer stopping distance
- Brake pad thickness below 2 mm (replace at 3 mm or less)
- Vibration through the brake pedal (may indicate scored disc)

## Tools needed

- Jack and axle stands (or vehicle ramps)
- Wheel brace (to remove wheel bolts)
- 12 mm socket or spanner (caliper slide pin bolts)
- 17 mm or 19 mm spanner (wheel bolts)
- Brake caliper piston wind-back tool or large G-clamp
- Flat-blade screwdriver
- Wire brush (to clean caliper mounting bracket)
- Brake cleaner spray
- Torque wrench

## Tips

- Never press the brake pedal while the caliper is removed — the piston will shoot out.
- If the fluid reservoir is already at maximum, syringe some out before pushing the piston back — otherwise it will overflow and damage paint.
- If slide pins are seized (caliper slides on one pin only), the pad on one side will wear much faster than the other — always check both pins move freely.
- Bed-in (breaking-in) new pads is important — immediately doing hard stops with new pads can glaze the friction material.
- If the pads are at minimum and the discs are deeply grooved, replace both. Fitting new pads on scored discs reduces braking performance and causes accelerated pad wear.
