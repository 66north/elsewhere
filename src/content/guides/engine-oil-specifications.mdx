Engine oil is the lifeblood of your engine. Choosing the right grade, type, and capacity keeps the 4M41 diesel and 6G75 petrol running smoothly, protects against wear, and extends engine life. Both engines use the same oil grades but have different capacities. This guide covers oil selection, capacity, and change intervals for your Pajero IV.

## Tips

- Always check the oil level when cold (before starting the engine) using the dipstick. Top up if below the minimum mark.
- Change both oil AND filter together at each service interval. A clogged filter reduces oil flow and damages the engine.
- Never mix oil types or viscosities. Stick to the recommended grade for your climate.
- Diesel engines (4M41) produce more ash and sludge than petrol — the 15,000 km interval is firm. Shorter intervals (10,000 km) are recommended for severe driving.
- Petrol engines (6G75) are more tolerant of slightly extended intervals, but 15,000 km is the factory recommendation.
- Synthetic oils (5W-30) last longer than mineral oils. Most modern Pajero IVs run well on synthetics.
- Using the wrong oil grade or type can void your warranty and cause engine damage.
