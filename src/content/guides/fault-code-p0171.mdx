P0171 means the ECU's long-term fuel trim is adding more than ~10% extra fuel to compensate for a lean air-fuel mixture. The engine is getting too much air or too little fuel relative to what the MAF sensor expects. Common causes include vacuum leaks (unmeasured air), a dirty MAF sensor, or a weak fuel pump. This is a petrol-only code — the 4M41 diesel does not use the same fuel trim system.

## Symptoms

- Check Engine light illuminated
- Rough idle or hunting idle (RPM fluctuating)
- Hesitation on acceleration from low RPM
- Slightly poor fuel economy
- Engine may stall at idle when A/C is switched on

## Tips

- Start with a vacuum leak check — they are free to diagnose and cheap to fix. A cracked PCV hose is the most commonly missed cause.
- MAF sensor cleaning is a 10-minute job and worth trying before replacing the sensor.
- Do NOT use carburettor cleaner on the MAF sensor — it damages the hot-wire element. Use only MAF-specific spray.
- If P0171 appears alongside P0174 (Bank 2 lean), the cause is almost certainly a large vacuum leak or a bad MAF — both banks can't coincidentally run lean independently.
- A code that appears only after hard acceleration may indicate a weak fuel pump unable to maintain pressure under demand.
