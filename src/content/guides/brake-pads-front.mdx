Front brake pads on the Pajero IV typically last 40,000–60,000 km depending on driving style. They must always be replaced as a complete axle set (both sides). The process requires winding back the front caliper pistons — a simple piston push-in type, not screw-in.

## Symptoms

- Squealing or squeaking noise under braking
- Grinding metal-on-metal sound when braking
- Longer stopping distances than usual
- Brake warning light illuminated
- Pad friction material worn to less than 2mm
- Brake dust visible inside wheel dramatically reduced (worn pads produce less dust)

## Tools needed

- 17mm socket (wheel bolts)
- 12mm + 14mm socket (caliper bolts)
- Caliper piston compression tool (C-clamp or dedicated tool)
- Wire brush (for caliper slides)
- Brake cleaner spray
- Torque wrench

## Tips

- Always replace pads as an axle set (both sides). Replacing one side creates uneven braking.
- Remove about 20–30ml of fluid from the reservoir before compressing pistons to prevent overflow.
- Clean the caliper slide pins and regrease them — seized pins are a common cause of uneven wear and poor fuel economy.
- Bed in new pads with 8–10 moderate stops from 60 km/h before any hard braking.
- Do not apply grease to the friction material — only to backing plate edges and slide pin contact points.
