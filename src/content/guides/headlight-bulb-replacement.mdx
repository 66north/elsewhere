The Pajero IV uses halogen headlights as standard, with HID (xenon) as an option on higher trim levels. Most bulbs can be replaced from behind the headlamp housing without removing it. HID headlights use a D2S discharge bulb — do not touch the burner with bare hands, and note that HID systems carry dangerous high voltage even with the ignition off. Indicators and tail/brake lights use standard bayonet bulbs accessible from inside the boot or behind trim panels.

## Symptoms

- Headlight not working on one side (single bulb or fuse failure)
- Both headlights out — check fuse or relay, not bulbs (rare for both to fail together)
- Indicator flashing faster than normal (hyper-flash) — bulb in that circuit has failed
- Reversing light not working — check bulb and reverse switch
- Fog light not working — separate fuse circuit

## Tools needed

- Plastic trim clip removal tool (for boot liner on tail light access)
- Gloves (for halogen bulb handling)

## Tips

- Never touch a halogen or HID bulb glass with bare fingers — skin oils cause hot spots that shorten bulb life dramatically. Use gloves or a clean cloth.
- HID headlights: the system stores charge for several minutes after power is removed. Disconnect the battery and wait 5 minutes before handling the ballast or igniter. The discharge voltage is lethal.
- Headlight access on the Pajero IV: reach in from behind the headlamp unit — there is a rubber dust cap over each bulb. Twist and remove the cap, then disconnect the wiring plug, twist the bulb retaining ring, and pull the bulb out.
- Indicator hyper-flash (fast flashing): the indicator relay senses circuit current — a blown bulb in the front or rear circuit reduces current and triggers fast flash. Find and replace the failed bulb before the rapid flash misleads you into replacing the relay.
- Tail light and brake light access: open the tailgate, fold back the boot liner at the rear corners, and remove the plastic nut fasteners to access the lamp cluster from inside.
- Front fog lights: accessible via a cutout in the front bumper — no bumper removal needed on most Pajero IV models. Reach in from below.
- If an indicator bulb is replaced but hyper-flash continues, check the earth connection at the lamp housing — poor earths cause false low-current readings at the flasher relay.
