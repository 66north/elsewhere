The Gen 4 Pajero 3.2 Di-D (4M41 common rail) turbocharger operates with variable-geometry (VGT) boost control. Correct boost pressure is essential for performance, fuel economy, and preventing over-fuelling or under-fuelling. Low boost causes power loss and black smoke; over-boost triggers the wastegate fault code P0049 (Code 49 in blink-code diagnostics) and can indicate a stuck VGT. This page covers the factory boost pressure specifications and how to diagnose a boost problem.

## Symptoms

- Significant power loss under load, especially uphill or towing
- Black smoke from exhaust during acceleration
- Check Engine Light with code P0049 (over-boost) or boost sensor codes
- Turbo spools up but no increase in power after a certain point
- Turbo whistle that is unusually loud or high-pitched

## Tips

- A split intercooler hose is the most common cause of a sudden power loss on the 4M41. Pull over and check all hoses before assuming a turbo failure.
- Oil in the intercooler (visible black oily residue inside the hoses or intercooler tank) is normal in small quantities — it comes from crankcase blow-by. Large quantities indicate a failing turbo oil seal.
- VGT actuators on older Gen 4 trucks can stick from carbon and rust. Some owners clean and lubricate the actuator mechanism to restore free movement.
- After any intercooler hose repair, the vehicle needs to relearn boost control — a short drive with several full-throttle accelerations will help the ECU adapt.
