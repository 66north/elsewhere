P0128 means the engine is not reaching normal operating temperature within the expected time, or is running cooler than the thermostat's rated opening point. The most common cause by far is a stuck-open thermostat, which keeps coolant circulating through the radiator constantly and prevents the engine from warming up fully. Symptoms are subtle — heater that doesn't get hot, poor fuel economy, and a temperature gauge that never reaches the midpoint.

## Symptoms

- Check Engine light illuminated
- Heater not producing hot air (engine runs cold)
- Temperature gauge stays low or never reaches midpoint
- Poor fuel economy (cold engine runs richer)
- Slightly rough idle (fuel trims not optimised for cold engine)

## Tips

- A stuck-open thermostat is much more common than a stuck-closed one. The spring mechanism wears open, not shut.
- Thermostats are a wear item — replace every 100,000 km or when diagnosing a cold-running engine.
- Always drain enough coolant before removing the thermostat housing — usually 1.5–2 litres is enough to prevent coolant spillage.
- After replacing thermostat, run engine with heater on full and bleed air from the cooling system (look for air purge screw on thermostat housing).
- If you notice the temperature gauge sitting just below midpoint but the heater works well, the car is likely fine — check gauge calibration before replacing parts.
