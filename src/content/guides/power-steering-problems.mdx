The Pajero IV uses hydraulic power steering with a belt-driven pump and a steering rack. Common problems are fluid leaks (low fluid causing whine), a worn pump, and rack seal failure. Power steering fluid is rarely changed but should be inspected at every service — dark or burnt fluid indicates oxidation and accelerates pump wear. Heavy or stiff steering is a safety concern and should be addressed promptly.

## Symptoms

- Whining or groaning noise when turning the steering wheel
- Steering heavier than normal, especially at low speed
- Power steering fluid level dropping regularly (leak)
- Fluid puddle under the engine bay (usually front-left area)
- Steering wheel vibrating or shuddering when turned
- Fluid foam or bubbles visible in reservoir (air in system)

## Tips

- Check the power steering fluid reservoir first — it is a small cap-and-dipstick unit near the firewall. Level should be between MIN and MAX with the engine warm.
- Whining that increases with steering input usually means low fluid. Top up and check for leaks before assuming pump failure.
- Inspect the power steering hoses (high-pressure from pump, low-pressure return) for weeping at connections — these are the most common leak points.
- The power steering rack seal is a common failure point on 100,000+ km vehicles. Oil seeping from the rubber boots on the steering rack confirms rack seal failure.
- Do not overfill the reservoir — foaming from overfill causes air entrainment which accelerates pump wear.
- A pump that whines only on cold startup and quiets when warm usually just needs fresh fluid. A pump that whines constantly with correct fluid level is worn.
- Power steering fluid is Dexron II/III ATF or Mitsubishi Genuine PSF — do not use DOT brake fluid or any other type.
