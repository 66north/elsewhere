P0087 means the ECU detected fuel rail pressure below the commanded target. On the 4M41 common-rail diesel, this is a serious code — insufficient fuel pressure causes hard starting, loss of power, and can leave you stranded. Most common causes are a clogged fuel filter, weak high-pressure fuel pump, or a leaking high-pressure injector. Address immediately.

## Symptoms

- Hard to start or long cranking before firing
- Loss of power under load, especially above 2000 RPM
- Black or white smoke from exhaust
- Rough idle or engine cutting out
- Check Engine light illuminated
- DPF regeneration failing (insufficient fuel pressure)

## Tips

- Always replace the fuel filter before diagnosing further — it is cheap, takes 30 minutes, and fixes this code in 40% of cases.
- Do not run a common-rail diesel on low fuel — the fuel itself lubricates the high-pressure pump. Repeated low-fuel operation accelerates pump wear.
- Air in the fuel system causes the same symptoms. After filter replacement, prime the system by cycling the ignition 3–4 times before cranking.
- If you've had contaminated fuel (water or wrong fuel type), drain the tank and flush the entire system before replacing the filter — the contamination will destroy the new filter quickly.
