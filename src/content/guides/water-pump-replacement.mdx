The water pump circulates coolant through the engine block, radiator, and heater. Most pumps are belt-driven by the serpentine belt and last 80,000–120,000 km before the bearing wears out or the seal fails. A failed pump causes coolant leaks, overheating, and in severe cases, catastrophic engine damage (warped head, cracked block). While the job is moderate difficulty, it requires draining coolant, removing the serpentine belt, and careful reinstallation of gaskets.

## Symptoms

- Coolant leak from the front of the engine (pooling under the radiator)
- Overheating or slow warm-up (pump not circulating coolant efficiently)
- Grinding or squealing noise from the front of the engine (bearing wear in pump shaft)
- White crusty buildup around the pump housing (coolant seeping past the seal)
- Engine coolant temperature gauge slowly creeping up despite normal ambient temperature
- Sweet smell from engine bay (coolant leak)

## Tools needed

- Socket set and wrenches for serpentine belt pulley bolts (typically 10-14mm)
- Gasket scraper or plastic scraper to remove old pump gasket (plastic recommended)
- Serpentine belt tool or reference to check belt condition and routing diagram
- Coolant drain pan with minimum 2 liters capacity
- Torque wrench with 20-100 Nm range for pump bolts and pulley

## Tips

- Before removing the water pump, inspect the serpentine belt. If it is cracked, frayed, or glazed, replace it at the same time. The labor cost is minimal compared to pulling the engine again
- The water pump routing is critical. Take a photo of the belt routing BEFORE removal so you can replicate it exactly. Incorrect routing causes overheating and belt slipping
- Some engines (especially the 6G75 petrol) have the thermostat housing bolted directly to the water pump. If removing the housing, you may disturb the thermostat. Have a fresh O-ring seal on hand
- The water pump bearing can seize if the vehicle is stored in a cold climate and not started for weeks. If the bearing is stuck, do not force it to rotate by hand. Soak the pump in penetrating oil (WD-40, Liquid Wrench) for 15–30 minutes, then try again
- After the first 100 km of driving, recheck the coolant level and inspect the pump area for leaks. The gasket may need additional torque or bedding-in time
- If the engine still overheats after pump replacement, the thermostat or radiator may be the culprit, not the pump. Have the thermostat checked (see thermostat-replacement.yml)
