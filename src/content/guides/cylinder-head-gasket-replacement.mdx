Head gasket failure is uncommon on the Pajero IV but can occur after overheating events or at very high mileage (200,000+ km). The 4M41 diesel and 6G75 petrol both use multi-layer steel (MLS) head gaskets. Diagnosis is more important than replacement — head gasket work is a major workshop job, and many symptoms attributed to the head gasket have other causes (coolant loss is usually a hose or cap; white smoke is often diesel injection). Confirm thoroughly before condemning the gasket.

## Symptoms

- White smoke from exhaust that persists after engine is warm (coolant burning)
- Milky or mayonnaise-like contamination on the oil filler cap (coolant in oil)
- Coolant level dropping regularly with no external leak found
- Bubbles visible in coolant reservoir when engine is running (combustion gases)
- Engine overheating without obvious coolant loss or hose failure
- Oil contaminated in coolant reservoir (oil floating on top)

## Tips

- Check the oil filler cap first — a small amount of white condensation inside is normal in a cold climate. Thick, creamy, mayonnaise-like substance throughout the cap and filler neck is a genuine concern.
- A chemical block test (combustion gas test kit, available at auto stores) is the most reliable non-invasive test. Add the tester fluid to the coolant reservoir; if it changes from blue to yellow, combustion gases are present.
- White smoke on cold start that clears within 2–3 minutes is condensation — normal. Persistent white smoke after the engine is warm and up to temperature is the diagnostic sign to watch.
- If coolant is disappearing but there is no external leak, no white smoke, no milky oil, and no bubbles in the reservoir — suspect the heater core before the head gasket.
- On the 4M41 diesel, white smoke at idle after warmup is more likely an injector leak-off problem than a head gasket — check injector return lines first.
- Head gasket replacement requires removing the cylinder head, checking the head surface for warping (maximum 0.05 mm flatness deviation), and resurfacing if needed. This is a workshop job.
- If the engine has overheated, do not continue running it — coolant loss from a blown gasket leads to rapid cylinder wall and bearing damage.
