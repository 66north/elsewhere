The 4M41 diesel uses a timing chain rather than a belt, so there is no scheduled replacement interval. However, the chain tensioner is a wear item — a failing tensioner causes chain rattle on start-up (especially cold) and, left untreated, can allow the chain to jump timing with catastrophic results. Chain rattle that clears after 30 seconds of idle is the classic symptom. Replacement is a significant job requiring front engine access but is straightforward for a competent home mechanic.

## Symptoms

- Rattling noise from the front of the engine on cold start, clearing within 10–30 seconds
- Rattling that gets louder over time or takes longer to clear
- Timing chain noise at idle that does not clear (advanced wear)
- Check engine light with camshaft/crankshaft correlation codes

## Tools needed

- Socket set and ratchet
- Torque wrench
- Drain tray for coolant / oil
- RTV sealant if required by gasket type

## Tips

- If you hear chain rattle only at cold start that clears quickly, the tensioner is losing oil pressure overnight. Replace soon but it is not an immediate emergency.
- Continuous or worsening rattle requires prompt attention — a jumped chain can bend valves on the 4M41.
- While the front cover is off, inspect the chain guide rails for wear and replace if the plastic surface is worn through to metal.
- Change the engine oil and filter after this job — metal debris from a worn chain or guides may be in the oil.
