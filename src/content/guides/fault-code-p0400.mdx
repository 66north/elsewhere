Code P0400 indicates the EGR (Exhaust Gas Recirculation) system is not flowing exhaust gas back into the intake as intended. The EGR system reduces emissions by recirculating a small amount of exhaust gas, which reduces combustion temperature and harmful pollutants. A stuck or blocked EGR valve, failed EGR solenoid, or carbon buildup can trigger this code. This is a complex diagnosis requiring careful testing.

## Symptoms

- Check Engine light illuminated
- Rough idle
- Hesitation or stumbling during acceleration
- Loss of power under load
- Ping or knock sound during acceleration (EGR system failing to cool combustion)
- Slightly higher exhaust temperature
- Fault may be intermittent on first startup

## Tips

- EGR problems are common on high-mileage vehicles (100,000+ km) due to carbon accumulation.
- A stuck EGR valve can sometimes be freed by running a fuel system cleaner additive through the tank; not always effective but cheap to try first.
- Do NOT attempt to remove the EGR valve without draining coolant first — the cooler is integrated with the cooling system.
- If code returns after repair, carbon may be building up again. Quality fuel and regular fuel injector cleaning help prevent recurrence.
- EGR problems can cause pinging during acceleration — do NOT ignore this as it can damage the engine if prolonged.
- Some older Pajeros have a history of EGR solenoid failures. If replacement fixes the code, monitor for recurrence at future services.
