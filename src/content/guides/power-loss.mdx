If your Pajero hesitates, feels sluggish, or won't accelerate above 3000 RPM, the engine is likely in limp-mode or suffering from fuel/air restriction. Power loss can be caused by fuel starvation, air intake blockage, clogged catalytic converter, transmission slipping, or engine timing retard. Diagnosis requires checking fuel pressure, airflow, and scanning for fault codes.

## Symptoms

- Engine feels weak or underpowered, especially under load
- Slow acceleration or inability to reach normal RPM
- Engine cuts power at a specific RPM (often 3000-4000 RPM, limp-mode threshold)
- Check Engine light illuminated
- Black smoke from exhaust (overfueling), white smoke (lean or coolant), or blue smoke (oil burn)
- Transmission seems to slip or not hold gears (but may be normal if engine is weak)
- Hesitation when merging on highway or climbing hills

## Tools needed

- OBD2 scan tool to read fault codes
- Fuel pressure gauge (to check fuel system)
- Vacuum gauge (to check exhaust backpressure)
- Air filter (for inspection/replacement)
- Flashlight and mirror (to inspect filter and hoses)

## Tips

- Fuel octane rating matters on the 6G75 petrol. Using 87-octane fuel in a vehicle tuned for 91-octane causes knock and power loss. Switch to premium fuel and retest
- Turbo lag is normal on the 4M41 diesel; low-RPM power is weak until boost builds. If this feels unusually sluggish, suspect boost leak
- Transmission fluid condition directly affects feel of acceleration. Even if engine is strong, a worn transmission feels powerless
- If power loss appears suddenly after a gas station fill-up, suspect bad fuel or water in tank. Drain tank and refill with fuel from a different station
