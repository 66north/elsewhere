A strong battery is essential for starting your Pajero IV and powering ancillary systems. Battery capacity (Ah) and cold cranking amperage (CCA) must match your engine's demands. Oversized batteries can overload the alternator; undersized batteries cannot start the engine in cold weather. This guide covers battery selection by engine type.

## Tips

- Always disconnect the negative terminal first before working on electrical systems — this prevents accidental short circuits.
- Invest in a quality battery from a reputable brand (Exide, Lucas, Bosch, Yuasa). Cheap batteries fail prematurely.
- Have your alternator tested if the battery frequently dies or requires charging. A failing alternator cannot keep the battery charged.
- Clean battery terminals every 6 months, especially in humid or coastal climates where corrosion is rapid.
- In very cold climates, use a battery tender (float charger) during winter storage to keep the battery fully charged.
- Never add distilled water to modern sealed batteries — they are maintenance-free.
- If the battery is more than 3 years old and won't hold a charge, replace it rather than trying to revive it.
