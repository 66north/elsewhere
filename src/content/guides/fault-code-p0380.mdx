P0380 indicates a fault in the glow plug pre-heat circuit on the 4M41 diesel engine. Glow plugs heat the combustion chamber to aid cold starting — without them, a diesel engine is very difficult to start below 5°C. The fault may be in the glow plug relay, the glow plug controller module, the wiring to the plugs, or one or more failed glow plugs. This is a diesel-only code.

## Symptoms

- Check Engine light illuminated
- Difficult cold starting (especially below 5°C)
- White smoke on startup (unburned diesel due to cold combustion)
- Rough idle until engine warms up
- Glow plug light on dashboard stays on longer than normal or does not illuminate

## Tips

- Replace all 4 glow plugs as a set even if only one has failed. The others are the same age and will follow shortly.
- Glow plugs are prone to seizing in the cylinder head after years of heat cycling. Apply penetrating oil (WD-40 or Plus Gas) and let it soak for 30 minutes before removal.
- If a glow plug snaps during removal, the remaining stub must be drilled and extracted — a workshop job.
- The glow plug relay is often mounted in the engine bay fuse/relay box. It is cheap and worth replacing if you cannot identify which plug is failing.
- Glow plugs do not affect warm starting — if the car starts fine when warm but struggles in cold mornings, P0380 is the likely culprit.
