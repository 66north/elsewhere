The Pajero IV is rated for 3,000 kg braked towing — one of the highest figures in its class. However, the towing limits involve several interacting figures (GVM, GCM, towball mass) that are easy to exceed without realising. Exceeding any one of them voids insurance and may be illegal. This guide covers the key numbers, what they mean, and what to check before towing.

## Tips

- Braked towing capacity: 3,000 kg — the maximum trailer weight when the trailer has its own brakes. This applies when towing at 3,000 kg with a maximum towball mass (TBM) of 180 kg.
- Alternative rating: 2,500 kg braked with a towball mass of up to 250 kg. If your trailer tongue weight exceeds 180 kg, you must stay within the 2,500 kg trailer limit.
- Unbraked towing capacity: 750 kg — the maximum trailer weight when the trailer has no brakes. Most trailers over 750 kg are required by law to have brakes fitted.
- Towball mass (TBM): the downward force the trailer exerts on the tow ball. Too little TBM causes trailer sway; too much overloads the rear axle. Aim for 8–10% of trailer GVM. On a 2,500 kg trailer that is 200–250 kg — check your trailer.
- GVM (Gross Vehicle Mass): 3,030 kg — the maximum total weight of the Pajero including passengers, fuel, luggage, and towball download. Towball download counts toward GVM. With a full load of passengers and gear, it is easy to exceed GVM before you even hitch the trailer.
- GCM (Gross Combination Mass): 6,030 kg — the maximum combined weight of the loaded Pajero and loaded trailer. GCM = your Pajero's actual weight + trailer's actual weight. Do not confuse GCM with towing capacity.
- Trailer brakes required: trailers over 750 kg unloaded (or over 2,000 kg loaded, jurisdiction-dependent) must have brakes. Over 2,000 kg, all trailer wheels must have brakes and an automatic breakaway system is required.
- Tow hitch rating: the towbar fitted to the vehicle must be rated to at least match the trailer weight. Check the towbar's compliance plate — some aftermarket towbars are rated lower than the vehicle's factory limit.
- Mirrors: if the trailer is wider than the vehicle, extended towing mirrors are legally required in most jurisdictions. Standard Pajero IV mirrors are adequate for most trailers up to 2.3 m wide.
- Electrical: the Pajero IV uses a 7-pin flat trailer plug (Australian standard) or 13-pin Euro connector depending on market. Confirm your trailer plug matches before hitching.
